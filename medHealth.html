Medicine & Allied Health

Medicine involves preventing, diagnosing, alleviating, and treating diseases with curative drugs to maintain health. It is vital in health science and biomedical research, focusing on non-surgical treatment methods. Internal medicine, a key branch, provides comprehensive care for adults, covering cardiology, endocrinology, gastroenterology, geriatrics, and more, offering in-depth expertise for managing complex health issues.
Internal medicine is crucial in medical education, being a mandatory part of Bachelor of Medicine and Surgery programs worldwide. Despite its challenges, the medical profession is highly rewarding. The ultimate goal of medicine is to improve health and save lives, highlighting its profound impact and noble nature. This dedication to well-being and alleviating suffering motivates many to pursue a career in medicine, despite its demands.
